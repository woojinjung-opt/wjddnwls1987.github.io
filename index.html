About

Research summary

I study mesoscale microstructure evolution in metallic and dispersion nuclear fuels, with an emphasis on diffusion-driven phenomena, constituent redistribution, and grain growth inhibition. My work combines phase-field modeling, the MOOSE framework, and research-oriented numerical implementation in Fortran 90 and Python.

Selected projects

U–10Zr Metallic Nuclear Fuel — Constituent Redistribution

Completed · 2025
  • Developed a MOOSE-based phase-field model to simulate U–Zr constituent redistribution incorporating porosity and sodium infiltration.
  • Aligned simulations with experimental trends and authored a peer-reviewed article.
  • Conducted in collaboration with KAERI (Korea Atomic Energy Research Institute).

U–7Mo Research Reactor Fuel — Grain Growth with Zener Pinning

Ongoing
  • Investigating precipitate-induced grain growth inhibition during coating processes via phase-field modeling.
  • Performing parametric studies in precipitate volume fraction and radius to evaluate Zener pinning effects on growth kinetics.
